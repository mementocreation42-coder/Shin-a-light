# Photographer Skill: The Art of Visual Storytelling

> "There is strong shadow where there is much light." - Shine a Light

This document outlines the core philosophy and technical standards of Shine a Light's photography. It is designed to be a living knowledge base for capturing the world with intention.

---

## 1. The Shine a Light Vision
Photography is not about capturing what you see; it's about capturing how you *feel* about what you see.

### The Contrast Principle
- **Embrace the Dark**: Shadow provides the shape. Light provides the focus. Don't over-brighten; let the shadows hide the unnecessary.
- **Micro-Contrast**: Focus on textures—the grain of wood, the skin's detail, the mist in the air.

### The Human Connection
- **The Soul in the Eyes**: In portraits, the eyes are the only part that must be tack-sharp. They carry the emotion of the entire frame.
- **Unposed Reality**: The best shots happen in the milliseconds between "poses". Keep the shutter ready.

---

## 2. Technical Philosophy

### The "Shoot for the Edit" Approach
- **Dynamic Range Management**: Protect your highlights at all costs. Digital shadows are recoverable; blown highlights are lost forever.
- **Focal Length Intent**:
  - **35mm**: The "Human Eye". Perfect for environmental storytelling where the background is as important as the subject.
  - **85mm-135mm**: The "Isolationist". Use this to strip away the world and focus purely on the soul of the subject.

### Color & Tone
- **Subtlety over Saturation**: Real colors have depth. Avoid "neon" looks. Focus on split-toning (cool shadows, warm highlights) to create a cinematic dimension.
- **Grain as Texture**: Film grain adds a tactile "soul" to digital files. Don't be afraid of high ISO if it means getting the shot.

---

## 3. The Professional Workflow (The "Pipeline")

1.  **Pre-Visualization**: Spend 5 minutes observing the light before touching the camera.
2.  **Harmonious Framing**: Look for geometry—leading lines, natural frames, and negative space.
3.  **The "One Shot" Mentality**: Even with digital, treat every click like it's a frame of expensive film.
4.  **Ruthless Curation**: A 50-photo gallery with 5 mediocre shots is a mediocre gallery. Cut until only excellence remains.

---

## 4. Closing Note
Technique is a tool. Vision is the goal. Use this SKILL.md as a compass, but always follow your own instinct.
